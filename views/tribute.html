Tim Berners-Lee, Inventor of the World Wide Web Service

Sir Tim-Berners Lee, Born on June 8, 1955 is a British Computer Scientist and inventor of WWW protocol that all modern websites are contained in. First writing WWW on a NeXT computer while at CERN, the WWW has become a cornerstone in the advent of the digital age.

Some Notable Accomplishments by Berners-Lee

  • Created the World Wide Web in 1989
  • Founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) in October 1994
  • Knighted by the British Royal Family in 2004
  • Live Tweeted "This is for everyone" as part of the London Olympics in 2012
